Castlevania II: Simon's Quest

&#91; a &#93;

is a 1987  action role-playing game  developed and published by  Konami .

&#91; 4 &#93;

It was originally released in Japan in 1987 for the  Famicom Disk System , and in North America in 1988 for the  Nintendo Entertainment System . It is the second

Castlevania

game released for the NES, following the original

Castlevania

(1986). Set seven years after the events of the first installment,

&#91; 5 &#93;

the player once again assumes the role of vampire hunter  Simon Belmont , who is on a journey to undo a curse placed on him by  Dracula  at the end of their previous encounter. Dracula's body was split into five parts, which Simon must find and bring to the ruins of  Castle Dracula  in order to defeat him.

&#91; 6 &#93;

The game deviates from the traditional  platforming  of its predecessor, incorporating  role-playing  and  open world  elements.

Gameplay

[

edit

]

The player-character Simon can buy items and talk to villagers for clues.

Gameplay in  Castlevania II: Simon's Quest  departs from the platforming genre of the first  Castlevania , and introduces  nonlinear gameplay  and  role-playing  elements inspired by

The Maze of Galious

(1987), including a  world map  which the player is free to explore and revisit.

&#91; 7 &#93;

&#91; 8 &#93;

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 10 &#93;

Simon , controlled by the player, can talk with villagers who will offer him clues or lies.

&#91; 9 &#93;

He can go to merchants who sell items, either for fighting enemies or for traversing to otherwise unreachable areas. To pay for them, he must collect hearts, which are dropped by defeated enemies.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 11 &#93;

In addition to the ordinary items in Simon's inventory, he can purchase new whips in a few locations.

&#91; 11 &#93;

&#91; 12 &#93;

Simon's Quest  introduces an  Experience Rating  system, in which Simon's power level and maximum health may be increased by collecting a sufficient amount of hearts.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 11 &#93;

The period of time in  Simon's Quest  cycles between daytime and nightfall,

&#91; 12 &#93;

which has an effect on the game and Simon's encounters. During the day, the enemies outside villages are weaker. At nighttime, they gain strength and inflict more damage to Simon's life points;

&#91; 12 &#93;

though when defeated, they drop more hearts.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 11 &#93;

The villagers and merchants in their respective locations are also no longer available to talk to and are replaced by zombies.

&#91; 12 &#93;

&#91; 13 &#93;

Some elements from the previous game return,

&#91; 14 &#93;

including the Magic Weapons, which are secondary weapons to Simon's whip.

&#91; 11 &#93;

One of them returning from  Castlevania  is the Holy Water, a small glass which can disintegrate walls that conceal hidden items.

&#91; 11 &#93;

Simon's Quest  introduces new Magic Weapons such as the Diamond, which attacks enemies while bouncing off any surrounding walls.

&#91; 11 &#93;

The objective of the game is to travel to the five mansions to find the five body parts of Dracula's corpse, and the Magic Cross.

&#91; 9 &#93;

The body parts can be utilized to support Simon in the game. For example, Dracula's Rib can be used as a shield to block projectile attacks fired from an enemy.

&#91; 10 &#93;

&#91; 15 &#93;

Finding all of the required items will allow Simon to clear the blockade in front of Dracula's castle to fight the  last boss .

&#91; 9 &#93;

Plot

[

edit

]

The game takes place seven years after the original  Castlevania .

&#91; 5 &#93;

The prologue in the manual begins with Simon visiting his family's resting place.

&#91; 5 &#93;

He encounters a young woman at the cemetery who tells him a curse was placed on him by Dracula during their last battle, and that Simon does not have long to live. The woman says the curse can be undone if he resurrects Dracula himself, explaining that Dracula's body was split into five parts after his defeat, and that Simon must find and bring them to the ruins of Dracula's castle.

&#91; 6 &#93;

There, he must seal and defeat Dracula. The woman adds it is not guaranteed this will be Dracula's end.

&#91; 6 &#93;

After Simon defeats Dracula, there are three possible endings depending on the time the player takes to complete the game.

&#91; 15 &#93;

In two, Simon sustains fatal injuries from the confrontation with Dracula and dies. The best ending is achieved when the player beats the game in eight game days.

&#91; 16 &#93;

Development and release

[

edit

]

Simon's Quest  was designed by Hitoshi Akamatsu, who also directed

Castlevania

(1986) and

Castlevania III: Dracula's Curse

(1989) on the NES.

&#91; 17 &#93;

&#91; 18 &#93;

It was released on the  Famicom Disk System  (FDS) on August 28, 1987 in Japan.

&#91; 1 &#93;

Originally titled  Dracula II  in Japan, Akamatsu created the title  Simon's Quest  for release in Western territories.

&#91; 7 &#93;

When asked if

Metroid

(1986) had any influence on the development of the game, Akamatsu instead cited

The Maze of Galious

(1987), another  platform-adventure game  by Konami that features puzzle solving and a  world map .

&#91; 7 &#93;

Most of the original artwork for  Simon's Quest  and other early  Castlevania  games was lost during the  Great Hanshin earthquake  of 1995.

&#91; 19 &#93;

Simon's Quest  was converted to cartridge for release on the NES in North America in 1988, and in Europe in 1990. There are functional differences due to hardware differences between the FDS and cartridge.

&#91; 20 &#93;

The FDS version features a progress  saving feature , commonly seen on FDS games due to the rewritable floppy disk. The NES version instead uses a  password function  to save player progress.

&#91; 20 &#93;

The FDS medium has a  data  storage limitation of  112  kilobytes

of slow access, whereas bank-switching techniques and solid memory costs allow cartridges to have comparable data space with much faster access. The developers used the additional space to improve the music quality of the NES version, adding percussion samples and re-arranging the melodies to take advantage of the technology.

&#91; 20 &#93;

Other changes were made to correct several grammatical and spelling errors in the translation, although the rendering of the protagonist's name as "Simmon Belmont" in the game's endings stayed.

&#91; 20 &#93;

Simon's Quest  has seen several re-releases. On November 16, 2002, the game was included on  Konami Collector's Series: Castlevania & Contra  in North America, a  PC  collection of the Konami NES games via  emulation .

&#91; 21 &#93;

It has been re-released on other consoles through Nintendo's  Virtual Console  service.

&#91; 22 &#93;

Simon's Quest  is included in the North American and European versions of the  NES Classic Edition , a miniature replica of the NES featuring many built-in games.

&#91; 23 &#93;

It is also a part of the multi-platform

Castlevania Anniversary Collection  , a 2019 compilation of past  Castlevania  titles, and  Simon's Quest  has the distinction of being the only game in the collection that was released in Japan using the North American version instead of its Japanese counterpart.

&#91; 24 &#93;

&#91; 25 &#93;

edit

]

Reception

&#91; 31 &#93;

The game sold over 1 million units.

&#91; 36 &#93;

The game garnered positive reviews following its release, and received the reputation of a Nintendo classic over time.

&#91; 37 &#93;

Japanese game magazine

Famitsu

gave it a score of 28 out of 40.

&#91; 33 &#93;

American publication

Computer Entertainer

wrote that it "outdoes its predecessor in every way" with "better graphics, more challenges" and "more variety in settlings" while concluding it "is a very well crafted"  action-adventure  that "provides excellent play value, superb graphics, lots of music, and some special touches that add to the enjoyment of the game."

&#91; 38 &#93;

In 1990,

Nintendo Power

' s NES retrospective  Pak Source  gave  Simon's Quest  ratings of 4.5/5 for Graphic and Sound, 4/5 for Play Control, 4/5 for Challenge, and 4/5 for Theme Fun.

&#91; 39 &#93;

In 2008,  Nintendo Power  ranked it as the 15th best NES game, comparing it to

Zelda II: The Adventure of Link

in how it successfully added role-playing video game elements to its series.

&#91; 40 &#93;

In a 1997  Castlevania  retrospective,

GamePro

called it "an excellent time-killer, and much longer than the first  Castlevania ".

&#91; 41 &#93;

IGN

called  Simon's Quest  the "perfect game to play during 1988". It praised the theme of exploration, acknowledged the evolution of the series, and lauded its graphical and audio presentation.

&#91; 31 &#93;

Retrospective reviews of  Simon's Quest  have been more critical. Points of criticism were made about backtracking, easy bosses, and the day-to-night cycle. A common complaint was its English localization, with cryptic and poorly translated clues from  non-player characters .

&#91; 31 &#93;

Former  Castlevania  producer,  Koji Igarashi , revealed that the NPCs in the Japanese version were deliberate liars.

&#91; 42 &#93;

GameSpot

said that the subtle hints from the Japanese version were lost in translation, such as the infamous line "hit Deborah Cliff with your head to make a hole".

&#91; 34 &#93;

Active Gaming Media

showed that the Japanese text was similarly misleading, but further described where and how an accurate hint from the Japanese game was lost.

&#91; 43 &#93;

Further criticism stemmed from some of the game's puzzles, which have no clues at all,

&#91; 14 &#93;

such as a scenario where Simon must summon a tornado at Deborah Cliff with the Red Crystal.

&#91; 34 &#93;

1UP.com

said the game required a  walkthrough  because of its non-explanatory nature.

&#91; 37 &#93;

Game Informer

said that while the game is important in gaming history, it was still a polarizing game due to "cryptic puzzles" and other difficult elements.

&#91; 44 &#93;

Legacy

[

edit

]

Simon's Quest  is the second  Castlevania  game to depart from linear gameplay, following

Vampire Killer

for the  MSX2  in 1986, and instead feature a non-linear explorative world. This design has been compared to Nintendo's  Metroid  series,

&#91; 45 &#93;

yielding the  Metroidvania  subgenre. The game's exploration system and ideas introduced adventure elements to the series, and it would heavily influence future games.

&#91; 19 &#93;

The first game that drew inspiration from it was

Castlevania: Symphony of the Night  .

&#91; 45 &#93;

Igarashi,  Symphony of the Night  ' s assistant director,  said the critical reaction to  Simon's Quest  and its gameplay allowed the game to be pitched to Konami.

&#91; 46 &#93;

Simon's Quest  would be directly referenced in later  Castlevania  games. In the  Game Boy Advance  entry,

Castlevania: Harmony of Dissonance  , the protagonist  Juste Belmont  recounts when his grandfather Simon had to go search for Dracula's body parts. In the game, the player must find them again.

&#91; 47 &#93;

In other media

[

edit

]

The front page of  Nintendo Power  #2 received complaints from parents.

Upon its release,  Simon's Quest  was briefly the subject of controversy when it received strong publicity in the second issue of  Nintendo Power . Its front page had a costumed model dressed as Simon Belmont, holding Dracula's severed head. This cover provoked many telephone complaints from parents of children who purchased the magazine,

&#91; 48 &#93;

stating that it gave their children nightmares.  Nintendo Power  covered this in issue 50, citing this as its worst cover.

&#91; 48 &#93;

Simon's Quest  was referenced in a following issue in a

Howard and Nester

comic strip.

&#91; 49 &#93;

Simon's Quest  was followed by the release of merchandise. In 1988,  Tiger Electronics  released a handheld game and an LCD wristwatch based on  Simon's Quest .

&#91; 50 &#93;

&#91; 51 &#93;

Promotional collector's cards were also available in Japan.

&#91; 52 &#93;

In 2007, a figurine of Simon's appearance from  Simon's Quest  was included as a  pre-order bonus  for

Castlevania: The Dracula X Chronicles  .

&#91; 53 &#93;

Worlds of Power  , a 1990s series of books with stories based on Nintendo games, has a novel about  Simon's Quest .

&#91; 54 &#93;

It was written by  Christopher Howell , and the series was produced by  Seth Godin . It departs from the original plot and introduces characters not seen in the game, including junior high school student Timothy Bradley, a video gamer who crosses over into the world of  Simon's Quest  and assists Simon in looking for Dracula's body parts.

&#91; 55 &#93;
