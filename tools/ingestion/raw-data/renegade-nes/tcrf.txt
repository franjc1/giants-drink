Renegade

is a somewhat reimagined port of the influential  arcade game  of the same name. It was the first game in the Kunio-kun series, the first home console game developed by Technos Japan, and also one of the first beat-'em-ups  ever made .

Regional Differences

The game was significantly reskinned when brought to the US, similar to the original arcade release. The original Japanese game was localized a second time for the  Double Dragon & Kunio-kun Retro Brawler Bundle  and is now officially titled "Nekketsu Renegade Kunio-kun".

Title Screen

The title screen was spruced up for the US release. It shows a deteriorated wall with the game's title written in spray paint.

Introduction

The Japanese version  does  have a bit of an intro sequence. Kunio's friend Hiroshi casually walks across the screen and is abducted by a car that drives past him. Kunio chases after the car on a motorcycle, shouting "WAIT!"

The US version doesn't really have any story to speak of. The manual basically just says "you're surrounded by punks, beat them up."

Characters

Player

Japan

US

Info

Kunio / Mr. K  The player character was originally wearing a white Japanese school uniform. This was changed to a brown opened vest and brown pants, clearly inspired by the movie  The Warriors . His skin tone was also changed from yellow to pink, a change that was also applied to all the other characters.

Enemies

Japan

US

Info

The enemies of the first stage were changed from high school delinquents to... greasers?

Aside from a change in skin tone, these bosozoku gang members went untouched. Note that they didn't bother to fix the stray pixel to the right of his head.

The schoolgirls were completely redrawn and their uniforms were changed to red dresses.

Bosses

Japan

US

Info

Riki / Jack  Riki, Kunio's future rival in the series, wears an open blue school uniform with tape around his abdomen. Jack has long hair and wears an open vest with a headband.

Shinji / Joel  Shinji was changed from a sleazy-looking guy in blue to a scary-looking punker with a mohawk and ponytail... and some sort of weird thing covering his mouth.

Misuzu / Kim  Misuzu was originally a sukeban, the leader of a schoolgirl gang. Her school uniform was changed to what looks like a red cheongsam. She was also redrawn a bit.

Sabu  Sabu, the yakuza boss, had his outfit changed from brown to green.

Items

The health power-up was appropriately changed from a rice ball to an all-American hamburger. Amusingly, the game's manual shows a picture of the rice ball while still calling it a hamburger.

Mission 1

Japan

US

This level was changed from the outdoors Shinjuku Station to a shabby-looking underground subway. Surprisingly, signs advertising beer were added to the US release. How'd  that  get past Nintendo's censors?

In the Famicom version the backgrounds for the first two stages differ depending on the difficulty setting, going from daytime (blue skyline) on Level 1 (the easiest setting), sunset (orange skyline) on Level 2 and nighttime (black skyline) on Level 3 (the hardest setting). This effect is absent on Mission 1 of the NES version, due to the change from an outdoor setting to an indoor one, but is still present on Mission 2 (the only stage to go relatively unchanged between versions).

Ending

The door in the background opens. Hiroshi is standing there crying. He walks over to Kunio and they shake hands. Cut to credits. This sequence was completely removed from the US release; the game jumps to the staff roll immediately after the final boss is defeated.

Additionally, the theme that plays during the credits has a different intro in the US version.
