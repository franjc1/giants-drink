Stage Select

A stage select can be accessed when starting a new game by using  Pro Action Replay  (PAR) code

7E1D7E01  .

At one point, the game had a way to enable this by pressing  Up, Up, Down, Down, Left, Right, Left, Right, B, A  (likely at the title screen), at which point a sound effect would play and the stage select would take effect upon starting the game.

The programming for this button code still exists at SNES address

86DB4D

(US version), but is never referenced. The array of button values is stored at SNES address

81F6DC  .

(Source: Original TCRF research)

Minigame Menu

Entering  Elmyra, Shirley, Calamity  as a password will take you to a menu allowing you to play any of the between-stage minigames, and view the roulette wheel cutscene.

Difficulties

The US and European version removed the passwords from Normal difficulty, so they are only provided in and useable for Children difficulty.
They also include a Challenge difficulty, which is not available in the Japanese version. The levels are identical to Normal difficulty, but the hearts (health points) are reduced to only one and there are only three continues.
In the Japanese version, Normal difficulty has infinite continues, while the US and European version limits them to five.

Ending

The Japanese version had 2 versions of the ending screen depending on which difficulty mode the player selects. When beating the game on "Children" mode, Babs appears in the rings. When beating the game on "Normal", Buster appears. The US and European versions removed these and used the same background as the title screen for all difficulty settings, with "Children" and "Normal" featuring messages encouraging the player to replay the game on the next difficulty level. In "Challenge" mode, there is no message and it just shows "The End".

USA / Europe

The US and European versions added Judy Steel to the "Special Thanks" section of the credits.

Japan

USA / Europe

In the Japanese version, the credits in Normal difficulty are with backgrounds and animated characters, while in the US and European version, they are black just like the credits in kids mode. The animated credits will only show up upon clearing the added Challenge Mode difficulty in those versions.

Spanish Version

Besides the Spanish localization, a bug was fixed where Stage 2 could not be completed if the Dash button was reassigned.

Miscellaneous

To keep the  Back to the Future  joke, the speedometer that pops up during the cutscene at the end of the second stage is in kilometers per hour in the Japanese version and miles per hour internationally. The number on the speedometer itself was also made yellow, for some reason.
