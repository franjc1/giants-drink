Cool World

is an  action video game  developed by British studio Painting By Numbers. It was released by  Ocean Software  in 1993 for the  Super Nintendo Entertainment System . It is loosely based on the 1992 movie  of the same name .

&#91; 2 &#93;

Both the European and North American versions are in  English .

In contrast to the  computer/Game Boy  and  NES  versions, this version of the game plays more like an  adventure game . It is the only version of the game that allows players to control Jack Deebs, who must avoid the advances of Holli Would and return home.

&#91; 5 &#93;

Cool World  was first mentioned in issue #47 (April 1993) of the North American video gaming magazine

Nintendo Power  .

&#91; 6 &#93;

Gameplay

[

edit

]

Players must avoid The Popper Police or risk being arrested.

The player controls Jack, who must avoid being surrounded by the Popper Police, a cartoon police force, or get arrested.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Depending on how many times the player has broken the law and whether he has collected the in-game currency of nickels or not, the player will either get a lecture by Detective Frank Harris and/or be forced to pay  bail  money.

&#91; 8 &#93;

Ten coins is the usual bail required by Detective Harris. Players can visit Las Vegas to increase their nickel stash and store their coins at the Cool World bank.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Other in-game locales include a  pawn shop  that allows items to be traded or bought with nickels in addition to the Slash Club where the player arranges a date with Holli Would. At Holli's apartment building, the player must navigate its exterior in order to reach the date with Holli. If the player did not arrange a date with Holli before reaching the top, Jack is tossed back to the ground level. In the malt shop, candy can be found in addition to other sweets. However, entry is restricted to players who have collected several pieces of candy.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Reception

[

edit

]

Allgame  gave  Cool World  an overall rating of 2 stars out of an overall 5.

&#91; 9 &#93;

Nintendo Power  gave this game a rating of 3.25 out of 5. &#91;

citation needed

&#93;

Power Unlimited  gave this game a 75% rating in their July 1993 issue and concluded that: "The game around the movie is just as wacky, strange, and actually not really good either. What exactly you have to do is unclear, but it looks nice and it all sounds very funny."

&#91; 10 &#93;
