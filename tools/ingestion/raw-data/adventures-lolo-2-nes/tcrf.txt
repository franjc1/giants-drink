Regional Differences

Opening Screens

Japan

US

Europe

The Japanese and European versions have the early HAL logo, while the US version has the HAL America logo with a lighter blue background, the US and European versions also have copyright info displayed below the logo, while the Japanese version doesn't. The European version also changes the date from 1989 to 1990 which also applies to title screen. (see below)

Japan

US

Europe

Outside the Japanese version, the US and European versions add the number 2 gradually appearing from top to bottom animation, while it unused in the Japanese version, it still present inside the game's graphic data. A trademark is also added to the right of "LOLO" in the European version.

Demo

Japan

International

When waiting at title screen for seconds, the Japanese version goes to stage demo, while the International version goes to instructions demo.

Levels

In the Japanese version  Adventures of Lolo , all the levels are original, while in the  Adventures of Lolo 2 , just like its previous title to include previous games' levels, again taken from  Eggerland: Souzou e no Tabidachi  and

Eggerland: Meikyuu no Fukkatsu  , but this time not only 10 levels taken from the Japanese version to here albeit some levels placed in different order, but also have 6 completely new unique levels.
