A solid, technically impressive action game developed by Factor 5 for the  Turrican  series. Semi-notoriously got downsized from a 6mbit cartridge to a 4mbit one during development, leading to the game being somewhat unfinished. (It's still not as bad as

Super Double Dragon

in that regard, though.)

It's notably the only Turrican game to receive a release in Japan (at least until Turrican Anthology Vol. I and II came along).

In 2018, a fully restored, though still unfinished, version of the game, entitled  Super Turrican: Director's Cut , was bundled in alongside  the sequel  with the Super Nt, an FPGA-based reproduction of the SNES developed by Analogue.

To do:  Investigate  Super Turrican: Director's Cut , and find out what's unused in the original version. Given the development, there's bound to be at least a decent amount of stuff.

Development Info

Level Skip

During gameplay, pause and press  Right, Left, Down, Right, A, Start  to advance to the next level.

The pause input to skip levels is slightly different in  Super Turrican: Director's Cut , as you need to press and hold  L + R + Select  before unpausing.

Sound Test

While highlighting "Exit" in the Options menu, hold  L + R + X + A  and then press  Start .

Unused Music

BGM 11  in the Sound Test is an unused boss theme located after the Stage 3 themes, so it can be assumed it was meant to play in Stage 3 if there was at least one boss. This theme can be heard in

Mega Turrican

for its final boss, or the Stage 3 bosses in its Amiga version named  Turrican 3 .
