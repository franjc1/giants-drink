Super Castlevania IV

&#91; a &#93;

is a 1991  platform game  developed and published by  Konami  for the  Super Nintendo Entertainment System . It has been re-released multiple times, including for the  Super NES Classic Edition .

Super Castlevania IV  features expanded play control,  16-bit  graphics featuring Super NES's  Mode 7 , and a soundtrack featuring new pieces and arrangements based on previous  Castlevania  music. Following the same setting as

Castlevania

on the NES, the game takes place in 1691  Transylvania , where the vampire hunter  Simon Belmont  must defeat the vampire  Dracula .

&#91; 3 &#93;

&#91; 4 &#93;

The game received critical acclaim and is considered one of the  greatest video games ever made .

Gameplay

[

edit

]

The player-character Simon Belmont can use the whip to latch onto rings and swing over areas.

Super Castlevania IV  is a  platform game  where the player takes control of Simon through eleven levels.

&#91; 5 &#93;

&#91; 6 &#93;

Players begin the game with five  lives , and it ends in a  game over  once they have lost them all. The player will lose a life if all of Simon's health gauge is depleted, fall into a hole or if they do not finish the level within the time limit.

&#91; 6 &#93;

The health gauge can be restored through food items that can be dropped from candles and breakable blocks, or with the Magic Crystal, which is received after defeating the  boss  at the end of each level.

&#91; 6 &#93;

&#91; 7 &#93;

A  password  can be entered to continue the game.

&#91; 8 &#93;

With Simon's whip, players can attack enemies in eight directions with the use of the control pad.

&#91; 8 &#93;

&#91; 9 &#93;

By holding down the attack button, the whip will go limp and can be waved around with the control pad, which can be used to block projectiles and hit enemies (albeit for much less damage).

&#91; 9 &#93;

The length and power of the whip can be  increased  up to two levels by collecting an item called the Morning Star.

&#91; 7 &#93;

The whip is used for fighting and for latching onto rings to swing over areas that are too wide or dangerous for the player to jump across.

&#91; 9 &#93;

In addition to jumping, the player can control Simon to move while crouching.

&#91; 10 &#93;

Like its predecessors, players can use secondary weapons that consume Simon's hearts, which are dropped from candles and enemies.

&#91; 7 &#93;

&#91; 11 &#93;

The secondary weapons include an axe which can be thrown in an arc, a watch which stops all enemy motion and a dagger that can be thrown across the screen.

&#91; 11 &#93;

Collecting the items known as the Double and Triple Shots allows the player to throw secondary weapons up to three times in a row.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Development and release

[

edit

]

Super Castlevania IV  was directed by Masahiro Ueno (credited in the game as Jun Furano since Konami did not allow the use of real names at the time), who was also the main programmer.

&#91; 12 &#93;

His first 16-bit game, Ueno's team possibly started development on it during 1989. Ueno liked the original

Castlevania

for the  Nintendo Entertainment System  the most and wanted to make a pure action game that was similar to it.

&#91; 12 &#93;

Ueno only considers  Super Castlevania IV  a remake of the original  Castlevania  to some extent.

&#91; 12 &#93;

Early design documents depict the player-character in modern attire, since the setting was not decided at the time.

&#91; 13 &#93;

Many features in  Super Castlevania IV  were introduced to make it a less frustrating game for players, such as giving them more control over Simon when he walked up stairs.

&#91; 12 &#93;

Due to the team being small, everyone was involved with the design and some ideas came from the creative artists and others by the programmers' experiments. The team drew maps on paper and a lot was changed as the game was worked on.

&#91; 12 &#93;

Branching stages, previously seen in

Castlevania III: Dracula's Curse  , were once considered but not included in  Super Castlevania IV .

&#91; 13 &#93;

Mitsuru Yaida (credited as Yaipon) programmed Simon and also implemented the game's whip system, which was to introduce some new gameplay that was not possible on the NES.

&#91; 12 &#93;

This idea was once planned for the original  Castlevania  game.

&#91; 13 &#93;

As the enemy and boss programmer, Ueno conceived some rough ideas and additional programmers would implement more detailed boss patterns. Earlier bosses were designed to be easier so that players could discover weak points and effective weapons without retrying.

&#91; 12 &#93;

Ueno worked on both the Japanese and English versions of the game, with the latter featuring some instances of censorship.

&#91; 14 &#93;

He was asked to alter the color palette in the English version in order to remove some depictions of blood in stage eight.

&#91; 12 &#93;

The packaging artwork for the North American version was created by Tom duBois, who designed the packaging for many other Konami titles outside Japan.

&#91; 1 &#93;

The game was released in Japan on October 31, 1991 for the Super Famicom.

&#91; 15 &#93;

It was released in North America in late December 1991.

&#91; 16 &#93;

&#91; 17 &#93;

Factor 5  created a short demo of the game running on the  Mega Drive  and presented it to Konami. Konami was impressed but ultimately chose to keep all Mega Drive development in-house, and did not pursue porting the game to the system.

&#91; 18 &#93;

The game has been re-released on several platforms over the years. It was re-released on the  Virtual Console  in 2006 for the  Wii ,

&#91; 3 &#93;

in 2013 for the  Wii U ,

&#91; 15 &#93;

and in 2016 for the  New Nintendo 3DS .

&#91; 15 &#93;

&#91; 19 &#93;

In September 2017, it was included on Nintendo's  Super NES Classic Edition , a miniature replica of the Super NES featuring many built-in games.

&#91; 20 &#93;

&#91; 21 &#93;

The game is included in  Castlevania Anniversary Collection , a compilation of past  Castlevania  installments released on May 17, 2019 for  Nintendo Switch ,  PlayStation 4 ,  Windows , and  Xbox One .

&#91; 22 &#93;

Audio

[

edit

]

The music for  Super Castlevania IV  was composed by Masanori Adachi and Taro Kudo (credited as Masanori Oodachi and Taro respectively). Ueno wanted to make the environment of  Super Castlevania IV  more interactive and was proud of how the game's sound effects and music contributed to the atmosphere.

&#91; 12 &#93;

It was released on

Akumajō Dracula Best 2

in a compilation with

Castlevania: The Adventure

and

Castlevania II: Belmont's Revenge

' s music. On June 21, 2017, the game's music was released on  vinyl  by Mondo.

&#91; 23 &#93;

&#91; 32 &#93;

The game sold 800,000 cartridges worldwide.

&#91; 33 &#93;

Upon  Super Castlevania IV's  release, the game was acclaimed by critics.

Weekly Famicom Tsūshin

generally complimented the game with three reviewers praising the graphics quality and three praising the control and gameplay, specifically the ability to use the whip in different angles.

&#91; 26 &#93;

Nintendo Power

gave the game four overall scores of 4.0, 4.0, 4.5 and 4.5 out of 5. Among several items, the magazine cited the game's graphics, music, and action sequences as positives.

&#91; 34 &#93;

In 1994 the game was reviewed by  Sandy Petersen  in

Dragon

#209's "Eye of the Monitor" column.

&#91; 25 &#93;

In 1992,

Entertainment Weekly

wrote, "Vampire noir: dark, earthy colors; ominous, almost subliminal sound effects — and when your hero swings from chandeliers or dispatches monsters with his iron flail, a spine-tingling sense of impending doom."

&#91; 32 &#93;

Super Castlevania IV  has been praised in retrospective reviews as well. In a 1997  Castlevania  retrospective,  GamePro  said it "is still one of the all-time best games".

&#91; 35 &#93;

It was named by  Nintendo Power  in a 2006 issue as the 66th best game made on a Nintendo system. It was ranked 27 in the last issue.

&#91; 36 &#93;

Official Nintendo Magazine

placed the game at 70 on their list of the 100 best Nintendo games ever.

&#91; 37 &#93;

Game Informer

's  review opined that it "perfect[ed] the classic formula" due to its whip and less stiff gameplay. It also praised its use of  Mode 7 .

&#91; 38 &#93;

In a review for the release on the Wii U's eShop,

Nintendo Life

wrote "Unlike many of the other 16-bit platformers of the era, the game has a mature and distinguished feel to it", and concluded it was the best of the original  Castlevania  installments.

&#91; 5 &#93;

In 2018,

Complex

listed the game 13th on their "The Best Super Nintendo Games of All Time", writing: "Next to  Symphony of the Night , this is the best  Castlevania  game of all time. The control is perfect, the progression is perfect, and even the ramping up of the difficulty is flawless, as it gets difficult in all the right spots. Also, it has the best soundtrack ever."

&#91; 39 &#93;

In 1996,

GamesMaster

ranked the game 55th in its "Top 100 Games of All Time".

&#91; 40 &#93;

Several publications have lauded it as one of the  greatest video games of all time .
