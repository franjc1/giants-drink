Totally Rad

is a

Mega-Man -ish  action-platformer best known for its radical facelift from the unassuming Japanese title  Magic John  to the gnarly localization published elsewhere. Whether this change ultimately proved to be tubular or bogus is a topic of scholarly debate.

Both versions of the game would later be compiled and released internationally for the Nintendo Switch in 2025 as part of the publisher's  JALECOlle Famicom Ver.  line of re-releases.

Hidden Cheats

There are two hidden codes. One activates a level select/sound test, while the other shows the game's credits.

Level Select

On the title screen, press  Up, Up, Down, Down, Left, Left, Right, Right, Start . A menu will appear allowing you to skip stages, view cutscenes, and listen to music and sound effects.

Credits

Magic John

Totally Rad

On the title screen, press  Right, Left, Left, Right, Up, Down, Down, Up  on Controller 2, and then press  Start  on Controller 1.

While the credits work perfectly fine in  Magic John,  the scanline IRQ that changes the graphics bank and scrolls the credits was not moved to work with the larger logo in  Totally Rad,  causing the bottom of the logo to use incorrect tiles.

Unused Graphics

Magic John

Totally Rad

Both  Magic John  and  Totally Rad  contain graphics of the player character in a "Raise the roof" pose for both kneeling and standing on one foot, but this is never used. The spellcasting animation does use a "squashed" version of the pose as an in-between frame, but the rest of the animation sees John/Jake pointing with fingers rather than raising palms.
