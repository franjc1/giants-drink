McKids

Industry  Clothing

McKids  was a chain of  clothing  stores marketed to children and operated by the  McDonald's Corporation  through a partnership with  Sears .

&#91; 1 &#93;

&#91; 2 &#93;

&#91; 3 &#93;

Overview

[

edit

]

In July 1987, Sears began selling the McKids line of children's clothing.

&#91; 1 &#93;

In December 1988, Sears opened the first seven free-standing McKids stores.

&#91; 1 &#93;

&#91; 4 &#93;

In early 1991, all 47 free-standing McKids stores were closed.

&#91; 5 &#93;

The McKids line of clothing continued to be sold at Sears stores.

&#91; 5 &#93;

In 1997, McKids clothing began to be sold exclusively at  Wal-Mart .

&#91; 6 &#93;

McDonald's exclusivity agreement with Wal-Mart ended at the end of 2003, and in 2004 the McKids brand was launched internationally, with 15 stores being opened in China.
