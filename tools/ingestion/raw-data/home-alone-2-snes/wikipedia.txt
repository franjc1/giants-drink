Home Alone 2: Lost in New York

is an  action video game  based loosely on the  1992 film of the same name . It was published in 1992 for the  Nintendo Entertainment System  and  Game Boy , then in 1993 for the  Genesis ,  Super NES  and  MS-DOS .

&#91; 5 &#93;

The game is dedicated to Tom D. Heidt, a programmer who died shortly before it was released.

&#91; 6 &#93;

Gameplay

[

edit

]

The player controls Kevin McCallister as he makes his way alone through New York City. Locations include the Plaza Hotel, Central Park, Uncle Rob McCallister's townhouse, and the streets of New York. Kevin fights enemies using a Slide, Flying Fist and Super Flying Fist moves; for weapons, he uses a Dart gun. Using a Necklace will cause walking enemies to slip off the screen (referencing a scene where Kevin uses the  pearls  from a  necklace  to trip up his pursuers). Powerups include Pizza Slices, Whole Pies, Cookies, Bells, Candy Canes and After Shave.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Release

[

edit

]

The game was unveiled in October 1992.

&#91; 8 &#93;

The game was released in late 1992 for the NES and Game Boy. Versions for the Super NES, Genesis, and MS-DOS followed in 1993.

Home Alone 2: Lost in New York  was awarded Worst Sequel of 1992 by

Electronic Gaming Monthly  . They also awarded it Worst Movie-to-Game of 1996.

&#91; 14 &#93;

Legacy

[

edit

]

In June 2016, video game developer Frank Cifaldi found the NES version's  source code  on an old  hard drive  and released it to the public. He has stated the game's code was based on

The Simpsons  -licensed titles on the NES, also by Imagineering.

&#91; 15 &#93;

&#91; 16 &#93;

A sequel for the Game Boy version, entitled  Home Alone 2: Lost In New York: Kevin's Dream , was slated for release in 1994, as made by Unexpected Development, but it was cancelled before it came out. Unexpected Development would re-work the title to be based on the  Fox Kids  animated series

Bobby's World  , complete with  Super Game Boy  functionality, but a planned 1995 release by Hi Tech Entertainment also did not occur. The ROMs for both versions of the game were leaked online in September 2020.
