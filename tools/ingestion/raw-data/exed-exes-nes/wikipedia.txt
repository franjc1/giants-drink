Savage Bees  , released as

Super Floating Fortress Exed Exes

&#91; a &#93;

in Japan, is a 1985 vertically  scrolling shooter  video game developed and published by  Capcom  for  arcades . It was released on the  Family Computer  by  Tokuma Shoten  the same year as simply

Exed Exes  .

Savage Bees  was licensed to Memetron originally for its North American release.

It was ported to the  Famicom  by Micronics.

Reception

[

edit

]

Reception

Review scores

Publication  Score

Nintendo Life

(VC)

&#91; 4 &#93;

Famimaga

(FC) 15.27/30

&#91; 5 &#93;

In Japan,  Game Machine  listed  Savage Bees  as the 15th most popular arcade game of March 1985.

&#91; 6 &#93;

Fan reception for the Famicom version was mixed: readers of

Famimaga

voted to give  Exed Exes  a 15.27 out of 30 score.

&#91; 5 &#93;

A reviewer of

Gamest Mook

noted that the insect and skull-themed enemies, the inorganic backgrounds, as well as the mysterious and anxiety-inducing music posed a unique and exciting atmosphere. He also praised the mechanic of turning enemies into collectable fruit items as "the greatest attraction" for this title.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Legacy

[

edit

]

Savage Bees  was included with its original name on the

Capcom Generations 3

for the  Sega Saturn  and  PlayStation ,

Capcom Classics Collection

for the  PlayStation 2  and  Xbox  and  Capcom Classics Collection Reloaded  for the  PlayStation Portable . This game was released for the  Wii

Virtual Console  Arcade in Japan on September 21, 2010, and in the PAL region and North America in January 2011. In 2013, it was released on

Capcom Arcade Cabinet

for the  PlayStation 3  and  Xbox 360  as  Savage Bees . It is also available in

Capcom Arcade 2nd Stadium

for the  Nintendo Switch ,  PlayStation 4 ,  Xbox One  and  Microsoft Windows , and as one of the rotating arcade games in

Street Fighter 6

' s Game Center.

&#91; 8 &#93;

Colonel Issue appears as a cameo character in

Project X Zone 2

as part of  Captain Commando's  Solo Unit attack.
