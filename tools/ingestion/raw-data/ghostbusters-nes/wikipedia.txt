Ghostbusters

is a 1990  platform

shoot 'em up  video game developed by  Sega  and  Compile  for the  Sega Genesis .

&#91; 4 &#93;

It features an original story based on the

Ghostbusters  films ,

&#91; 5 &#93;

&#91; 6 &#93;

and is unrelated to the  1984  Ghostbusters  game  by  Activision .

&#91; 7 &#93;

The game was released in the United States in August 1990,

&#91; 1 &#93;

&#91; 8 &#93;

and was released in the United Kingdom later that year.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 3 &#93;

A Brazilian version by  Tec Toy  was released for the console in 1991.

&#91; 10 &#93;

Gameplay

[

edit

]

Ghostbusters  is a  platform

shoot 'em up  with  side-scrolling  gameplay.

&#91; 11 &#93;

&#91; 3 &#93;

The game's story involves ghosts terrorizing a city after an earthquake.

&#91; 12 &#93;

The game features a choice of three playable Ghostbuster characters from the films:  Peter Venkman ,  Ray Stantz  and  Egon Spengler .

&#91; 11 &#93;

The Ghostbusters each have their own traits relating to speed and shooting strength, and they are each animated with  oversized heads  meant to resemble their respective actor's likeness.

&#91; 5 &#93;

&#91; 7 &#93;

&#91; 12 &#93;

The player can crouch, jump,

&#91; 3 &#93;

and is equipped with a positron gun, which can be shot in all directions and is used to eliminate ghosts.

&#91; 11 &#93;

The player earns money by eliminating ghosts, and can use the funds to buy upgraded weapons through shops that appear in between levels.

&#91; 11 &#93;

&#91; 3 &#93;

Upgraded weapons include a bomb and a gun that emits flames.

&#91; 3 &#93;

&#91; 13 &#93;

The player can also purchase a shield that provides temporary invincibility.

&#91; 3 &#93;

Money is also used to buy food for health replenishment.

&#91; 11 &#93;

Ghostbusters  includes six levels,

&#91; 14 &#93;

&#91; 15 &#93;

including four which are set in haunted residential buildings and can be played in any order.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 4 &#93;

&#91; 12 &#93;

The objective of the Ghostbusters is to retrieve pieces of a stone tablet from each of the four initial levels by ridding the areas of ghosts.

&#91; 4 &#93;

"Middle ghosts" appear during the middle portion of a level and must be eliminated to progress to the main  boss enemy  at the end. Middle ghosts include  Slimer  from the films, while boss enemies include the first film's  Stay Puft Marshmallow Man .

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 11 &#93;

&#91; 3 &#93;

&#91; 6 &#93;

The player must capture each boss enemy after its defeat.

&#91; 6 &#93;

The fifth level, set in a medieval castle, only becomes available upon completion of the other levels, and is followed by a final battle.

&#91; 9 &#93;

&#91; 4 &#93;

&#91; 12 &#93;

The game features 22 selectable musical scores, including  Ray Parker Jr. 's song " Ghostbusters ".

&#91; 9 &#93;

Mean Machines

praised the introduction sequence, the graphics, and the variety of options. The magazine concluded that the game was "fairly enjoyable" and that it would likely appeal most to people who enjoy platform games.

&#91; 3 &#93;

Julian Boardman of

Raze

liked the graphics, but criticized the gameplay as repetitive and average.

&#91; 13 &#93;

The Games Machine

praised the playable character  sprites  for resembling their film counterparts, but criticized the simple and repetitive backgrounds. The magazine wrote that it was one of the better platform games available for the Genesis, but considered that it did not capture the spirit of the film.

&#91; 7 &#93;

Robert Swan of

Computer and Video Games

praised the graphics, including the playable character sprites. He also praised the in-game music, but criticized the game's version of Parker's "Ghostbusters" song. Swan considered the game addictive despite its difficulty.

&#91; 11 &#93;

Eugene Lacey of

ACE

praised the design of the game's ghosts, but believed that Parker's song had been rendered better in  other  Ghostbusters  games .

&#91; 9 &#93;

Mega Play's four reviewers gave very positive reviews unanimously to the graphics, diverse gameplay, the variety of enemies and the boss fights. One reviewer opined that it was one of the best games made for the Genesis.

&#91; 18 &#93;

In 2004, Ken Horowitz of Sega-16 wrote that it was one of the better movie-licensed games of its time, although he was disappointed by the minimum amount of visual effects, and said that the opening theme music was the major weakness of the game. Horowitz stated that the game had good graphics upon its release, but not as appealing by today's standards.

&#91; 5 &#93;

In 2008, Levi Buchanan of  IGN  said that the game was "pretty cool" upon its release, especially for  Ghostbusters  fans, but wrote that it was now something of a mediocre side-scroller, stating that the graphics were still adequate but that the gameplay was "fairly tame compared to what would come later" on the Genesis. Buchanan also questioned the absence of the Ghostbuster character  Winston Zeddemore .

&#91; 6 &#93;

In 2014, Robert Workman of  Shacknews  ranked it among the top three best  Ghostbusters  games. Although Workman believed that there were superior Sega platform games available at the time of its release, he stated that the game had an "innocuous charm to it".

&#91; 14 &#93;

In 2016, Luke McKinney of  Den of Geek  called the game "pretty decent" but ranked it among the weirdest  Ghostbusters  games, for its " macrocephalic " Ghostbuster characters and its absence of Winston.

&#91; 19 &#93;
